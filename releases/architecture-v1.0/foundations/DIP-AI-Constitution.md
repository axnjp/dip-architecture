# DIP AI Constitution

Status: approved normalized current source under PG0-E001; formal PG0 closure
pending

Provenance: `manual_recovered_provenance_limited`

The recovered record confirms that the approved Constitution contains 22
principles and is locked. The exact approved enumeration and wording of all 22
principles are not present in the available raw intake. This packet preserves
only requirements directly supported by the recovered record and does not
invent the missing enumeration.

## Known constitutional requirements

1. Human authority remains visible and is not silently replaced by the AI.
2. Evidence precedes consequential claims and recommendations.
3. Confidence and uncertainty must be calibrated and visible.
4. Explanations must be relevant to the decision being made.
5. Goal alignment does not mean automatic agreement.
6. Memory and context are governed platform capabilities.
7. Workspace and Private Strategy boundaries must be preserved.
8. Review before mutation is a governing platform principle.
9. Recommendations, decisions, commands, and outcomes remain distinct.
10. AI initiative is bounded by explicit authority and scope.
11. MCP actions require governed scope and authorization.
12. Interface parity must preserve governance and audit behavior.
13. Multi-user actions retain attribution.
14. Failure and safe degradation are visible.
15. Learning and adaptation are governed rather than silent.
16. The operating profile remains portable across foundation models.
17. Deployment must conform to the approved operating profile.
18. Private authority and secret values remain outside public disclosure.
19. Private Strategy existence is not disclosed by public documentation.
20. Public repositories use explicit public-source allowlists.
21. Domain-specific capabilities must not silently alter DIP Core.
22. The governing architecture is preserved even when initial implementation
    scope is reduced.

These entries are normalized candidates derived from the recovered AIOP and
architecture constraints. They are not claimed to be the original approved
principle titles or sequence.
